Marie Curie was born in Warsaw in 1867.
She studied physics and chemistry at the University of Paris.
Curie discovered two radioactive elements: polonium and radium.
In 1903, she won the Nobel Prize in Physics together with her husband Pierre Curie.
She later received a second Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1911, becoming the first person to win the award twice.
Her research contributed significantly to the development of cancer treatments.
Marie Curie died in 1934 due to aplastic anemia caused by prolonged radiation exposure.
